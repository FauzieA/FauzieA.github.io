Introduction to OPAAW

The Organisation of Islamic Cooperation's (OIC) Plan of Action for the Advancement of Women (OPAAW), adopted in 2008, is aimed at improving the status of women in its 57 member countries by advancing their rights and opportunities. This all-encompassing plan addresses key areas of women’s life outcomes, such as education, healthcare, economic participation, and legal protection.

In the field of education, OPAAW focusses on the importance of increasing women and girls' access to schooling and higher education. OPAAW encourages women to pursue education, in all its forms, to contribute more effectively to their families and broader society.

In the realm of healthcare, OPAAW calls for improved care for mothers and better access to essential medical services for women.

Another of OPAAW’s objectives concerns economic empowerment. OPAAW aims to facilitate women's entry into the workforce, encourage career development, and support female entrepreneurship.

Legal protection is also emphasised, and OPAAW encourages stronger laws against domestic violence, and legal reforms to ensure gender equality in aspects of women’s private and public lives.

Overall, OPAAW provides a framework and agreed-upon standard for OIC member countries to protect and improve the status of women where necessary. Crucially, it is entirely consistent with Islamic values and Shariah, as well as aligning with international standards. Member states are encouraged to develop national strategies based on this framework and report their progress regularly.
